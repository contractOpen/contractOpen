Virtual or physical? An object with human form, a mechanical heart — I do not think such a product is suitable to operate in spaces where humans gather socially.

After comparing OpenClaw and Mule, I've had some thoughts about future life and development.

Computing power should be provided by political organizations, managed and distributed by the Smart Agents. All important permissions should be executed through public voting. Special permissions — such as law enforcement, judiciary, military — should be handled separately.

The Smart Agent dispatches Mules to each individual citizen according to their role and position, replacing their work. At the same time, Mules are deployed for public service duties. For example: a farmer's Mule handles seed procurement, fertilization, irrigation, harvesting — fully automated facilities managed by your Mule. A street-sweeping Mule controls cleaning vehicles, garbage collection, waste processing, etc. Mules that serve individuals create value; those that serve the Smart Agent create computing power.

If a virtual Mule replaces your work, what are you in reality? Thinking? Creating? Wasting time? This might be a philosophical question.

How can VR enable a state of "mind-merging" between humans and Mules? How can the lifecycle of a personal Mule be deeply bound to its human? What will humans be doing then — in virtual or in reality?

"Through thought one attains; without thought, one attains nothing."

Now, let's start with some experiments! Before someone assigns you a Mule, create one first.
